    Learn Chess

    How each piece moves

    Pawn moves forward one square (two on its first move) and captures diagonally. Knight moves in an L-shape and jumps over pieces. Bishop slides diagonally any distance. Rook slides horizontally or vertically. Queen combines rook and bishop movement. King moves one square in any direction, and may castle once with a rook under strict conditions.

    Beginner tips

    Develop knights and bishops before your queen, castle early to protect your king, control the centre squares, and avoid moving the same piece twice in the opening without good reason.

    Opening principles

    Aim to occupy or influence the centre, develop pieces toward open lines, get your king to safety through castling, and connect your rooks once the back rank is clear.

    Common checkmate patterns

    Watch for the back-rank mate (a king trapped behind its own pawns), the smothered mate (a knight delivering mate to a king surrounded by its own pieces), and the ladder mate using two rooks or a rook and queen.

    FAQs

    Can I undo a move against the computer? Yes, use the Undo button at any time. How does castling work here?
